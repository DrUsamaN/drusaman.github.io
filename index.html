  • Tell Me More

How does one really understand Islām?

The most important sources for Islamic knowledge are of
course The Holy Qurʼān, The Word of Allāh, and well as The Sunnah
(which is a collection of words, actions, descriptions and silent
agreeing of The Messenger of Allāh ﷺ).


Many Muslims do not know Arabic though. It is therefore important
to give them some resources for them to learn Islām and to learn Arabic.
Helping with making these resources is what I aim to do In Shāʼ Allāh.

Here some of my projects and ideas:

Qurʼān translation

The first French word-by-word transtation of The Holy Qurʼān, wa-l-Ḥamdu Lillāh. Available online for free.

Color coding Qurʼān words

Most word-by-word tranlations don't actually translate each word separately, I have found some ideas to fix this issue In Shāʼ Allāh.

Color coding for colorblindness

Some information on how to do color coding in a way that will not only be accessible to people with normal eyesight but also those with colorblindness.
